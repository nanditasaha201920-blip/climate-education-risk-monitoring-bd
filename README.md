📌 Climate-Education Risk Monitoring System (CERMS-BD)

A Data-Driven Early Warning Framework for Climate-Induced Educational Disruption in Bangladesh

🌍 Project Overview

The Climate-Education Risk Monitoring System (CERMS-BD) is a data-driven prototype designed to identify and predict climate-induced risks to education systems in Bangladesh.

Bangladesh is one of the most climate-vulnerable countries in the world. Floods, cyclones, and environmental shocks regularly disrupt schooling, increase dropout rates, and push children into long-term vulnerability.

This project proposes a predictive, integrated monitoring system that combines climate, education, and socioeconomic data to enable early warning and targeted intervention.

🎯 Objective

To develop a scalable national framework that:

Identifies districts and populations at risk of climate-induced school dropout
Integrates multi-source data for predictive analysis
Generates early warning alerts for policymakers and NGOs
Supports targeted, data-driven interventions
🧠 System Architecture

The CERMS-BD system is built on four key layers:

1. Data Integration Layer
Climate data (flood, cyclone, rainfall)
Education data (attendance, dropout, school closures)
Socioeconomic data (poverty, child labor, displacement)
2. Risk Analysis Engine
Composite risk scoring model
Weighted indicators across climate, education, and vulnerability
3. Early Warning System
Risk classification (Low, Medium, High)
Automated alerts for high-risk regions
4. Decision Support Layer
Policy insights for intervention
Resource allocation guidance
NGO and government coordination
📊 Dataset Description

Due to the absence of a unified national dataset linking climate, education, and socioeconomic indicators, this project uses a preliminary integrated dataset.

The dataset is constructed using:

Publicly available statistics
Climate vulnerability patterns
Evidence-based assumptions reflecting regional realities in Bangladesh
Key Variables:
Climate: flood risk, cyclone risk, salinity risk, rainfall
Education: attendance rate, dropout rate, school closure days
Socioeconomic: poverty rate, child labor rate, displacement
⚙️ Methodology

The system applies a multi-dimensional risk scoring model:

Climate Risk Score
Education Disruption Score
Socioeconomic Vulnerability Score

These are combined into a total risk score, which classifies districts into:

🟢 Low Risk
🟡 Medium Risk
🔴 High Risk
🚨 Early Warning Output

Example output:

⚠ Satkhira → HIGH RISK (Immediate intervention required)
⚡ Khulna → MEDIUM RISK (Monitoring required)
✅ Jamalpur → LOW RISK
